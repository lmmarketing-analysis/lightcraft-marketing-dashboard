Consultant's Recommendations & Key Questions

A comprehensive, proactive plan to maximize your profitability across all channels.

Strategic Rationale: The "Why"

Your SEM is your financial anchor with room to grow. Your Search Engine Marketing is a proven success. You will protect and optimize this profitable asset by reallocating budget to top performers and improving campaign efficiency.


Social Media has untapped potential. The high click rates prove people are interested in your ads, especially during sales. You must now bridge the gap between interest (clicks) and profit (sales). The problem isn't the ad platform; it's the pathway from ad to a completed sale that's broken.


The power of creative and landing pages. You need a clear understanding of which ads and which "off-ramps" (your landing pages) actually convert clicks into customers. You need to find the winning combination.

Action Plan: The "How"

For Social Media Marketing (SMM): The "Test & Learn" Approach


Reduce to a "Test & Learn" Budget: Reduce the SMM budget to a manageable amount, like $500 per month, to be used exclusively for targeted tests. This minimizes financial risk while you find a profitable formula.


Run Sprint Tests on Ad Creative & Audiences: You will run short, focused tests to quickly find what works. You'll create and test high-quality video ads against existing formats and test new audience segments to find your most ideal clientele.


Audit the "Off-Ramps" (Landing Pages): You must urgently audit the pages your ads are sending people to. Is the page loading fast? Is it easy to buy? Does the message match the ad? The campaign is failing at this stage, and fixing it is critical for conversions.


For Search Engine Marketing (SEM): The "Optimize & Protect" Approach


Demand a Conversion Tracking Audit: This is non-negotiable. Before any new campaigns, work with your agency to separate and track the value of each conversion action (e.g., phone calls, sales). This will provide you with the true data needed to measure ROI.


Reallocate Budget from Underperformers: Shift money away from generic and less-efficient competitor keywords to your top-performing brand and product keywords. This will make your current campaigns even more profitable.

Refine Ad Creative and Messaging: Even with strong performance, there's always room for improvement. Create new ad variations that highlight specific benefits like "Free Delivery" or "60% Off" to improve your click-through and conversion rates.


Adopt Generative Engine Marketing (GEM): Your agency should explore how to make your brand visible in AI-powered search results and citations. This new form of search engine presence is a key area for growth and protecting your brand from emerging competitors.


For Performance Max (PMAX): The "Strategic Pilot"


Pilot with a Clear Goal: Launch PMAX only after conversion tracking is fixed. Use a small budget as a test, with the goal of finding new customers who are similar to your most profitable existing customers.


Important: Untapped Market Opportunity

What is Generative Engine Marketing (GEM)?

In today's digital world, AI tools like Gemini and ChatGPT are becoming "answer engines," not just search engines. People are asking them questions like, "What's the best light fixture for my bedroom?" and the AI gives them a direct, curated answer. **GEM is the strategy to ensure your brand is cited in those answers.**


Why is GEM a "Gold Rush"?

This is a new and underpriced opportunity. While competitors are still focused on traditional search rankings, brands that get cited by AI are getting high-quality customers who are ready to buy. This is because AI traffic is often more engaged and comes from users who have already made up their minds. The brands that move first will win the largest share of this new, highly profitable traffic.


How to Start Winning with GEM:

You need to create content that is easy for AI to "lift" and "cite." This means structuring your content for clarity with: **clear summaries, bullet points, structured comparisons (like pros and cons), and by directly answering high-intent questions** your customers are asking before they buy. The easier you make it for the AI to recommend you, the more likely you are to get the customer.

Expected Outcomes & Metrics

By following this plan, you will have a more profitable, focused, and data-driven marketing strategy. The ultimate goal is to discover a profitable formula for social media while simultaneously making your core SEM channel even more efficient.

Key Metrics to Track (Simplified)

Cost Per Action (CPA) on Social Media: This shows how much money it costs to get a new customer or lead from social media ads. You'll use this to find the cheapest way to get new business.


Landing Page Conversion Rate: This tells you what percentage of people who visit a specific page actually take the desired action, like buying something or filling out a form. You'll track this to fix any "off-ramps" where people are leaving without converting.


Search Ad Conversion Rate & ROI: This measures how many people who click on your search ads become customers, and how much profit you're making from the money you spend on those ads. This helps you see if your search advertising is actually making you money.

Key Questions for the Agency

Before you can finalize your strategy, you need clear answers from the agency on these critical points.

1. What is a "Conversion"? The SEM report bundles different actions (catalog downloads, phone calls, orders) into one number. You need a breakdown of this to understand which actions are most valuable. You also need to know why the social media campaigns show zero "Place Order" conversions. Is their tracking method different, or is the campaign simply not working?

2. Are you getting online sales from your campaigns? The SEM report shows 17 "Place Order" conversions in July. Do these represent direct online purchases? If so, you need to know the revenue from these sales to calculate your true Return on Investment.

3. What exactly are you targeting in the "Competitors" campaign? To decide if this budget is well-spent, you need a list of the specific keywords being targeted.
